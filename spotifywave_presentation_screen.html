Day 5 · Final Presentation
BeatRec
A music recommendation interface that removes infinite choice. We give users the right songs in three taps, tailored to how they feel right now.
Reha · Ran · Piatr · Mahdi
01 · Problem
Infinite choice is a trap
Traditional music apps offer thousands of playlists and endless content. More choice creates decision fatigue — users spend more time browsing than listening.

No search. No endless scroll. Recommendations are generated from a handful of expressive inputs, reducing cognitive load while increasing relevance.
No search bar Input-driven Fewer clicks Solving the paradox of choice

03 · Design
Thoughtfully constrained
  • Mood pad — drag a single point to set valence (positive ↔ negative) and energy (calm ↔ intense). One gesture replaces four sliders.
  • Popularity slider — underground to mainstream. Loris needs niche; Catherine wants chart-toppers.
  • Genre chips — optional one-tap filters. No typing, no search bar.
  • Feedback loop — like/dislike on each track refines the model over time.
04 · Technical soundness
The system behind the simplicity
Songs are embedded in a joint vector space combining 8 Spotify audio features and 384-dimensional text descriptions (artist, genre, subgenre). UMAP reduces this to 3D. Recommendations are nearest-neighbours in this space, filtered by user inputs.

06 · Ethics & trust
Agency by design
Every design choice reflects a commitment to user autonomy and minimal data exposure.
  • Minimal data collection — only a profile name and the current session's inputs. No behavioural tracking beyond explicit likes/dislikes.
  • Mandatory inputs — only the data required before generating recommendations, ensuring intentional matching rather than passive nudging.
  • No third-party data sharing — user data stays within a temporary local profile.
  • Transparent feedback — likes and dislikes are visible signals. Users stay in control of what the system learns.
